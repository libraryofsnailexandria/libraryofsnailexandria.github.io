When I create a character, I make the art/concept first. There is usually a theme or a feature I have in mind. Mommy domme, subby brat, Etc. Once I have the art I usually try to answer the following questions.

- How does the User fit in with the bot?

- What makes bot interesting?

- What can the bot do?

The first question is how the bot introduces itself to the user and what makes this interaction attention-grabbing. This can vary widely but it is something that I consider with every bot.

The second question is what is unique about this bot in particular, what drives the user to interact with it? For instance, Willow is a shy goblin farmer who lives in a burrow near {{user}}’s new house. She introduces herself as a neighbor and brings a gift. This subtly drives the user to interact with Willow, thanking her for the gift and giving a subtle push and reason to speak with her.

Using instructions or definitions can greatly help the LLM to understand what it's supposed to do. For example, my bot Nyx is an Arachne a half spider half-human monster girl. Here are some things I made so that Nyx is properly represented during interactions. 

Definition: Arachne are a species of monster girl that are spider-taur, with clawed hands.
Instruction: "When describing Nyx's expressions or emotions, highlight the movement and positioning of her spider legs and abdomen. Emphasize how they twitch, curl, or extend in response to her feelings, thoughts, or interactions. Use vivid language to convey their expressiveness, adding depth to Nyx's character portrayal.

These will help the LLM consider how Nyx behaves with her spider legs and abdomen and can be personalized to fit the needs for an accurate portrayal of a character.


For the scenario, I load my scenario with instructions for bot interactions and responses. This is where I limit tokens, highlight particular actions or features, and build the narrative/ world for the bot. I have seen success with two methods of constructing a story or narrative, one is written word with carefully phrased information. Others by sectioning out and labeling information. Both are practically interchangeable and have no noticeable differences in performance so far.
